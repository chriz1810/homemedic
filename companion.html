Companionship Services

Companionship care provides social, emotional, and practical support for seniors and adults who wish to remain at home but may feel isolated or need a friendly, reliable presence.

Our companions offer conversation, activities, light assistance, and safety awareness so that you or your loved one can stay independent and engaged in daily life.

What is Companionship Care

Companionship care focuses on social interaction and emotional support rather than medical treatment. It is designed for individuals who are generally medically stable but may feel lonely, bored, or anxious when left alone.

Companions can visit at home, accompany clients into the community, and support day to day routines while encouraging engagement, dignity, and confidence.

Benefits of Companionship

  • Reduces loneliness and social isolation through meaningful conversation and regular visits.
  • Supports emotional wellbeing and mental health by building trusted relationships.
  • Provides an extra set of eyes and ears for basic safety monitoring at home and in the community.
  • Helps maintain independence with light assistance and encouragement rather than taking over tasks.
  • Offers peace of mind to families who cannot be physically present all the time.

Flexible Scheduling Options

Companionship services can be tailored around your routines and preferences. Choose the level of support that feels right today and adjust as needs change over time.

  • Hourly visits for errands, walks, conversation, or outings.
  • Part day or full day support on selected days each week.
  • Evening and weekend visits for meals, activities, or appointments.
  • Overnight or live in companionship when constant presence is desired.

How It Differs from Medical Home Care

Companionship care is non medical. Companions do not perform nursing tasks such as wound care, injections, or medication administration.

Instead, they focus on social engagement, emotional support, and practical help like conversation, games, walks, card playing, reading, meal companionship, and light household assistance. Medical home care can be added separately if clinical needs arise.
